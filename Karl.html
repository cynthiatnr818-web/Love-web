A Letter to Mr. Karl

Written on a night when I was thinking of your guidance

Dear Mr. Karl,

You are the best teacher I have ever met. I've said this many times, and every time I mean it from the bottom of my heart. You're not the kind of teacher who just sticks to textbooks. What you've taught me goes far beyond the scope of physics.

Thank you for firmly telling me when I doubted whether I was suitable for math: "Your rigor and curiosity make you a born physicist." Thank you for seriously telling me it was dangerous when I tried to manipulate others' emotions. Thank you for stopping me with a smile without embarrassing me when I spoke carelessly.

Thank you for showing me that a good teacher can be both a strict mentor and a friend. When the Oxford rejection letter came, you said you'd prepare tissues if I cried. But you know what? The strength you taught me didn't let me cry for long.

You changed my way of thinking, broadened my horizons, and more importantly‚Äîyou showed me what an educator should be like. I will always remember what you said: "BNDS is a school, not an application factory." I will carry everything you taught me and continue moving forward.

Your forever student,

Cynthia
